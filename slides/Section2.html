Modeling, Hierarchical Models, and Bayesian Inference

"...all models are wrong; the practical question is how wrong do they have to be to not be useful." - George Box (1987)

  • All models are simplifications of reality, but we must be careful not to oversimplify.
  • Hierarchical models allow us to break a difficult problem down into component parts.
  • The fundamental building block of hierarchical models is conditional probability, and appropriate probability distributions.
  • Bayesian inference is a particularly flexible tool for HM.

Probability Distributions

  • Probability distributions can have different interpretations, here we prefer to consider them a way of quantifying uncertainty about unknown quantities.
  • Consider that probability distributions depend on parameters. We can think of such a distribution as being "conditional" on some fixed value of those parameters.
