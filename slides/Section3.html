Novel Developments in SEIR(S) Models

Spatial Generalization - Motivating Assumptions

  • The number of contacts between individuals in a spatial location follows a Poisson distribution.
  • When individuals travel, their contact behavior is well modeled by the contact behavior in their destination.
  • Contact beteen spatial locations can be expressed as a weighted sum of a number of "distance" metrics.

Novel Developments in SEIR(S) Models

Spatial Generalization

Based on these assumptions and their associated parameters, we can derive a parametric form for the exposure probability at a particular time point and spatial location, which is required to construct the HM. The result is a flexible parameterization that can acommodate numerous population structures.

Details on the derivation are available in this document, but are beyond the scope of this talk.

Novel Developments in SEIR(S) Models

Intensity and Reinfection Processes

  • Generalization of intensity process structure has some history in the literature (i.e., Lekone and Finenstadt, 2006), though it is generally presented differently for each problem. We have developed a general structure to tackle a wide variety of hypotheses, as well as to provide appropriate temporal bases for prediction in the absence of full information about disease behavior and control efforts.
  • The same structure is employed for reinfection, allowing the study of pathogen mutation and waning vaccine efficacy among other things.
