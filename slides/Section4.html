Case Studies

The 1995 Ebola Outbreak in Kikwit, Congo

  • Kikwit is a large city in the Bandundu region of DRC.
  • There were a total of 316 documented cases of Ebola.
  • The epidemic began in march, and ended in July.
  • Control efforts begain in mid May and continued until the epidemic was contained.
  • This data has been well studied (Chowell et.al. 2005),(Lekone and Finkenstadt 2006), so it provides a good test of new methods.

The 1995 Ebola Outbreak in Kikwit, Congo

  • The effect of the intervention is immediately obvious from the raw case counts.
  • Lekone and Finkenstadt employ a two part intensity process: an overall intercept, and a linear time term starting after the control efforts begin.

The 2014 Ebola Outbreak in West Africa

  • Unlike the previous case, this epidemic is ongoing and does not have the benefit of a single intervention in a single spatial location.
  • We use the previous observations about R0 and R(EA) to compare several models of varying flexibility, though we must use spatial formulations.
  • As a comprehensive intervention timeline has not been compiled, we use basis splines to motivate the intensity process.
  • The chosen spatial structure provides a separate parameter for traffic between each pair of countries.
