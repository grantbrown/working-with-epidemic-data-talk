Working With Epidemic Data

Challenges and Opportunities in a Rapidly Changing Analytics Landscape

What is an epidemic?

  • "excessively prevalent", "characterized by very widespread growth or extent" [1]
  • Here we're focused on infectious disease epidemics
  • There is a lot of diversity in epidemic behavior: Flu, AIDS, Chikungunya, Ebola

Important Challenges in Epidemic Data Analysis

  • Complicated disease processes
  • Correlated data, over both space and time
  • Incomplete information
  • Novel data sources
  • Computationally intensive tools


Important Challenges in Epidemic Data Analysis

Beyond the inherent complexity, the infectious disease landscape is changing:
  • Societies are increasingly connected
  • Changes in farming scale and technique present unique challenges
  • Climate change affects disease spread in a number of ways, both directly and indirectly


Potential Impact

  • Quantify pathogen behaviors: does a particular epidemic resemble well understood previous examples?
  • Detect anomalies: has a pathogen become more dangerous? Was a particular community resistant to disease spread?
  • Prediction: what scale of intervention is likely needed?
  • Program evaluation: are public health interventions having an effect?
  • Intervention targeting: is there a particular location where efforts will be most effective?
